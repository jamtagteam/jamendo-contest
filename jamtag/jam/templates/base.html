JamTag

JamTag started out as an entry to the Jamendo contest. The whole idea is that everything can have hidden data associated with it. Something like hidden Markov models. That being said, we were thinking tags, but instead of tags, why not tracks, why not Jamendo? JamTag is a web service that can provide any web page with hidden metadata through a browser extension (currently only for Firefox). That way a web page can have something like an easter egg associated with it. Think wikipedia article for a certain genre of music having some tracks associated with it. Give the service a try. Download the extension or check out the source code on GitHub.

Developers

JamTag was developed in various places after hours with a lot of help from coffee and a good company. We are three friends from college each with their own interests and lives. Our names are Borislav, Vanja and Zlatko. We managed to survive these sprints through sheer enthusiasm and our only problem is that we are lacking time for proper development. Our language of choice was Python with Django framework and CanJS on client side for MV* needs. We used jPlayer and jQuery respectively.
